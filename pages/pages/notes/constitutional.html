CONSTITUTIONAL LAW

what You know About Constitutional Law ??

Constitutional law is the body of law that defines the structure, powers, and functions of government, as well as the rights and liberties of citizens, based on a nation's constitution. It involves interpreting and applying the constitution to ensure laws and governmental actions adhere to constitutional principles.

Constitutional law is the foundation of a nation’s legal and political system, defining the structure of government, the allocation of powers, and the rights of citizens. It is derived from a country’s constitution—a supreme legal document that establishes the framework for governance and limits state authority to protect individual liberties. Key Principles of Constitutional Law
Supremacy of the Constitution The constitution is the highest law, overriding any conflicting legislation or government action.
Separation of Powers Divides authority among three branches:
Legislative (makes laws)
Executive (implements laws)
Judicial (interprets laws)
This system prevents tyranny by ensuring checks and balances.
