INDIAN POLITY AND GOVERNANCE

what You know About Polity and Goverance ??

The Indian polity is a **federal democratic republic** governed by the **Constitution of India**, adopted on **26th January 1950**. It establishes a **parliamentary system** of government with a **President** as the ceremonial head and a **Prime Minister** as the real executive. India follows a **dual polity** structure—**Union Government** and **State Governments**—with a **separation of powers** among the **Legislature, Executive, and Judiciary**. The **Supreme Court** is the highest judicial authority, ensuring constitutional supremacy. The **Indian Parliament** consists of two houses: the **Lok Sabha (Lower House)** and the **Rajya Sabha (Upper House)**. Members of Lok Sabha are directly elected, while Rajya Sabha members are elected by state legislatures. India is a **secular, socialist, sovereign, and democratic** republic, guaranteeing **Fundamental Rights** (Part III) and enforcing **Directive Principles of State Policy** (Part IV). The **73rd and 74th Constitutional Amendments** introduced **Panchayati Raj** and **Municipalities**, strengthening grassroots democracy. The polity also features an **independent Election Commission**, ensuring free and fair elections, and a **rigid yet flexible** Constitution, amendable under **Article 368**. India’s federal nature is balanced with a **strong central bias**, especially during emergencies. Thus, Indian polity reflects a **unique blend of federalism, democracy, and constitutional governance**, adapting to socio-political changes while upholding the rule of law.

The Country is surrounded by the Bay of Bengal in the east, the Arabian Sea in the west, and the Indian Ocean to the south. Lying entirely in the Northern Hemisphere, the Country extends between 8° 4' and 37° 6' latitudes north of the Equator, and 68° 7' and 97° 25' longitudes east of it.

Southern India covers states like Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Kerala, Andhra Pradesh and Telangana with the union territories of Lakshadweep. and Puducherry. Over 19.31% of India’s total area is occupied by these states and union territories. Bengaluru, the capital of Karnataka, is known as the ‘Silicon Valley of India’ because it is India’s leading exporter in the IT sector. Most populated cities of South India are Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Chennai, Coimbatore, Cochin and Mysore Northeast India includes the seven sister states of Arunachal Pradesh, Assam, Meghalaya, Tripura, Mizoram, Manipur and Nagaland. The Himalayan state of Sikkim and Jalpaiguri Division are two important parts of northeast India. There is a great ethnic and religious diversity within the seven sister states. The Central zone of India primarily refers to Madhya Pradesh and Chhattisgarh.
