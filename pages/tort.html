Tort law is a branch of civil law that addresses wrongful acts or omissions causing harm or loss to individuals, leading to legal liability. Unlike criminal law, which punishes offenders for crimes against society, tort law provides remedies—usually monetary compensation—to victims for injuries, property damage, or other losses caused by another party’s negligence,

Tort belongs to the category of civil wrongs The basic nature of civil wrong is different from a. criminal wrong In case of a civil wrong, the victim will institute a suit for damage against the wrongdoer.
